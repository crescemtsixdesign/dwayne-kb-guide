⚡ Start here every session: 3-min warmup — jumping jacks, bodyweight squats, arm circles, leg swings. Just get blood moving. Then hit the circuit below.

Rule: Last 2 reps of every set should be genuinely hard. If they're not, go heavier or add a rep next time. Rest 60s between sets.

🏋️ The 5 Exercises

1. Goblet Squat 3 x 10-12 • 60s rest
Why: Main leg builder. Hits quads, glutes, core. Biggest muscle groups = most calorie burn per minute.
Hold bell by the horns at your chest. Elbows tucked. Sit back like you're reaching for a low chair. Heels down. Go below parallel if you can.
2. Single Arm Row 3 x 10-12/arm • 60s rest
Why: Biggest upper body muscle group (back). Counteracts desk posture. Unilateral forces core to work harder.
Hinge at hip. Brace free hand on thigh or chair. Pull bell to your hip, not armpit. Squeeze back at top. Don't twist your torso.
3. Overhead Press 3 x 8-10/arm • 60s rest
Why: Only vertical push in the program. Preserves shoulder strength and upper chest. Key for long-term shoulder health.
First: clean the bell to rack position. Watch the clean here if you're unsure. From rack (bell resting on forearm at chest), press straight up. Wrist straight. Don't flare elbows. Brace core.
4. Two-Handed KB Swing 3 x 15-20 • 45s rest
Why: Fat-loss weapon. Full-body explosive movement. Torches calories, builds hamstrings, glutes, lower back, grip. No separate cardio needed.
This is a hip hinge, NOT a squat. Hips go back, knees barely bend. Power comes from snapping your hips forward. Arms are just ropes — bell floats up from hip drive. Your back stays flat the whole time.
5. Reverse Lunge 2 x 8-10/leg • 60s rest
Why: Exposes strength imbalances. Builds hip stability. Easier on knees than forward lunges. Each leg works harder pound-for-pound.
Hold bell at chest (goblet position). Step back — don't step forward. Front knee stays behind toes. Both knees bend to 90°. Drive through front heel to return. Keep torso upright.

🧘 Pelvic Floor Stretching (Tue / Thu)

15-20 min. Deep breathing throughout. Go slow — this is recovery, not training.

Deep Squat Hold (Malasana) 2 min
Child's Pose — knees wide 2 min
Happy Baby — rock gently 2 min
Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor — squeeze back glute 90s/side
Figure 4 Glute Stretch (lying) 90s/side
Cat-Cow — 10 slow breaths ~2 min
⭐ Diaphragmatic Breathing — hands on belly, inhale belly rises 5 min

👆 The breathing exercise is the most important one. Inhale → belly expands → pelvic floor relaxes. Exhale slow. Do this daily even on KB days.
