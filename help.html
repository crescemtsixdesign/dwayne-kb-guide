Dashboard guide

How to use this dashboard

Use this page as the daily control panel. It is built to answer one question fast: are you on track today, and what is the next move?

Today snapshot

What this section means

This is your current status card. It shows the four things that matter most right now:

  • Workout — did you train or not?
  • Calories — were you on target or drifting?
  • Weight — today’s weigh-in plus the short note.
  • Sleep / energy — whether recovery is helping or hurting you.
Coach score

How to read the score

The score is a quick summary, not a medical or body-composition measure. It is shown as a number out of 100 so you can see the size of the win or the problem fast. Higher is better. The dashboard displays it as both a number and a bar.

  • 85+ — strong day
  • 70–84 — keep pushing
  • Below 70 — reset and recover
Quick actions

What the buttons do

  • Mark workout done — logs the session and bumps the score a bit.
  • Calories on target — marks the day as aligned with the plan.
  • Save weigh-in — saves your bodyweight on this device.

These buttons save locally in your browser. They are for quick self-checks, not permanent records.

This week

How to read the weekly card

This section uses a Sunday-start week. It resets on Sunday and runs through Saturday.

  • Workouts completed — how many sessions are logged for the current week.
  • Avg calories vs target — your calorie trend, not a one-off meal.
  • Weight trend — the direction of the scale, not one noisy weigh-in.
  • Adherence streak — how many days you’ve been consistent.
Coach notes

How to use the advice sections

  • Where it is slipping — the main problem I want you to notice.
  • One move for today — the smallest useful action to take now.
  • Weekly readout — the short trend summary tying the week together.

If you’re tired, do the next action. Do not overthink the whole week.

Best use

How to actually use this day to day

  • Morning — open the dashboard and see whether training happened or needs to happen after work.
  • After meals — check whether calories are still on target.
  • Night — save weigh-in and glance at the next action for tomorrow.
Freshness

How the page stays current

The dashboard refreshes automatically every minute so the current time, date, and week stay aligned even if you leave it open.
