🧘 Pelvic Floor Stretching

Dwayne • Tue / Thu • 15–20 minutes • relaxation first, control second

Main rule: this is not a max-Kegel workout. The goal is to downshift tension, breathe into the belly/ribs, and learn to relax the pelvic floor on command. If anything makes you feel more clenched, reduce the range, slow down, or skip it.

Tuesday — Relax + Lengthen

Best for: releasing tension, hip opening, better pelvic floor drop

Routine

  1. Diaphragmatic breathing — 10–15 slow breaths. Hands on belly/ribs.
  2. Deep squat hold / Malasana — 2 minutes. Support yourself if needed.
  3. Child's pose, knees wide — 2 minutes. Breathe low and slow.
  4. Happy baby — 2 minutes. Rock gently, do not force range.
  5. Half-kneeling hip flexor stretch — 90 sec/side. Squeeze back glute lightly.
  6. Figure 4 glute stretch — 90 sec/side. Keep jaw, abs, and hips relaxed.
  7. Cat-cow — 10 slow breaths. Move with the breath, not speed.
Cue: inhale = belly/ribs expand and pelvic floor drops. Exhale = slow release. Do not brace hard.

Thursday — Relax + Light Control

Best for: keeping relaxation while adding a small dose of control

Routine

  1. Diaphragmatic breathing — 8–10 slow breaths. Start by relaxing first.
  2. Happy baby breathing — 5–8 slow breaths. Let the pelvic floor drop on each inhale.
  3. Bridge + gentle contraction/release — 1 set of 5–8. Gentle only. Full release between reps.
  4. Pelvic rocks — 8–10 reps. Smooth motion, no hard bracing.
  5. Windshield wipers — 45–60 sec. Easy hip rotation.
  6. Butterfly lying down — 60 sec finish. End relaxed, not activated.
Back-off rule: if the light control work increases tension, skip it and repeat Tuesday's relaxation routine instead.

Busy Day Fallback — 2-Minute Reset

Use this when you do not have the full 15–20 minutes.
  1. Lie on your back or sit supported.
  2. One hand on chest, one hand on belly.
  3. Take 10 slow breaths: inhale through nose, belly/ribs expand; long easy exhale.
  4. On each inhale, think: drop / soften / unclench.
Minimum effective dose: do this instead of skipping. Two minutes keeps the habit alive.

Success Criteria

  • You feel less clenched afterward, not more tense.
  • You can belly breathe without bracing your abs.
  • You can intentionally relax on inhale.
  • You finish calm. This should feel like recovery, not another hard workout.
