Squat Fixes

Use this when squat mechanics feel off, your shins complain, or you need a fast reset before training.

5-min reset Squat cues Best swaps Pain rules
Bottom line: mild leg stiffness after a new KB session is normal. Front shin pain in goblet squats means regress the movement and clean up the pattern.

5-minute pre-squat reset

1) Ankle rocks
10 reps/side
Wake up the ankle before you ask for squat depth. Good first move if heels want to lift.
Open YouTube video
2) Bodyweight box squat
2 x 5 slow reps
Use a chair or box. Sit back, stay tall, and stop before pain shows up. This is the squat pattern reset.
Open YouTube video
3) Hip hinge drill
10 reps
Groove the hinge so the squat doesn’t turn into a knee-dominant mess. Also helps separate squat vs hinge in your head.
Open YouTube video
4) Paused goblet squat to box
1-2 light sets x 5
Last step before loaded squats. Light bell, controlled descent, brief pause, no depth chasing.
Open YouTube video

Squat cues

Do thisNot this
  • Feet about shoulder width
  • Toes slightly out
  • Whole foot planted
  • Chest tall
  • Hold the kettlebell close
  • Think sit back and down
  • Stop at the deepest pain-free depth
  • Knees shooting way forward
  • Heels lifting
  • Torso collapsing forward
  • Forcing depth just to look good
  • Pushing through sharp or pinpoint pain

Pain rules

What’s okay
Normal adaptation
Mild stiffness in the back of the legs after a new leg session usually just means the tissue got a stimulus it isn’t used to yet.
What is not okay
Regress immediately
Sharp, pinpoint, or worsening pain means stop, shorten the range, lighten the load, or swap the exercise.
Next session rule: keep the squat easy and clean until the pattern is there. Then add load.
