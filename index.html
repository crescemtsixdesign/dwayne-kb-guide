🏋️ The 5 Exercises

1. Goblet Squat 3 × 10-12 · 60s rest
Why: Main leg builder. Hits quads, glutes, core. Biggest muscle groups = most calorie burn per minute.
Hold bell by the horns at your chest. Elbows tucked. Sit back like reaching for a low chair. Heels down. Go below parallel if you can.
2. Single Arm Row 3 × 10-12/arm · 60s rest
Why: Biggest upper body muscle group (back). Counteracts desk posture. Unilateral forces core to work harder.
Hinge at hip. Brace free hand on thigh or chair. Pull bell to your hip, not armpit. Squeeze back at top. Don't twist your torso.
3. Overhead Press 3 × 8-10/arm · 60s rest
Why: Only vertical push. Preserves shoulder strength and upper chest. Key for long-term shoulder health.
First: clean the bell to rack position. Watch the clean here if needed. From rack (bell on forearm at chest), press straight up. Wrist straight. Don't flare elbows. Brace core.
4. Two-Handed KB Swing 3 × 15-20 · 45s rest
Why: Fat-loss weapon. Full-body explosive movement. Torches calories, builds hamstrings, glutes, lower back, grip.
This is a hip hinge, NOT a squat. Hips go back, knees barely bend. Power comes from snapping hips forward. Arms are just ropes. Back stays flat.
5. Reverse Lunge 2 × 8-10/leg · 60s rest
Why: Exposes imbalances. Builds hip stability. Easier on knees than forward lunges. Each leg works harder pound-for-pound.
Hold bell at chest. Step back — not forward. Front knee behind toes. Both knees bend to 90°. Drive through front heel to return. Keep torso upright.

🧘 Pelvic Floor Days

When: Tuesday & Thursday · 10-20 min total
Main goal: improve pelvic floor control and relaxation to support better sexual control.
Bias: Relax first. Light strengthening only after you can actually let the area go.

Read this first: Video 1 is your foundation. It is the down-training / relaxation day. Video 2 is the light control day. Do not turn Thursday into a hard ab workout or a max-Kegel session. If you feel more tight, urgent, or clenched afterward, back off and return to Day 1 only for a week.
Tuesday — Day 1: Relax + Lengthen 10-15 min
Use this as the main pelvic floor reset day. Focus on diaphragmatic breathing, pelvic floor drop, and hip opening. No active squeeze on the exhale.
Do this order:
1) Pelvic floor drop breathing — 10-15 breaths
2) Supported deep squat or happy baby — 10-15 breaths
3) Dynamic frog — 10 slow reps
4) Kneeling adductor stretch — 8-10 breaths each side
5) 90/90 hip switches — 6-8 reps each side

Cue: Inhale = ribs and belly expand, pelvic floor drops. Exhale = just let it recoil. No forced Kegel.
Thursday — Day 2: Relax + Light Control 10-15 min
Start with release work, then add a small dose of controlled pelvic floor strength. This day is not about going hard. It is about learning to contract and fully release.
Do this order:
1) Stir the pot — 45-60 sec each direction
2) Happy baby breathing — 5-8 slow breaths
3) Bridge + gentle contraction — 1 set of 5-8 reps
4) Leg lowers or hover slides — 1 set of 5/side
5) Windshield wipers — 45-60 sec
6) Pelvic rocks — 1 set of 8-10 reps
7) Butterfly lying down — 60 sec finish

Rule: Every contraction is gentle and controlled. Full release between reps. If this makes you feel more tense, cut the strengthening in half or skip it.
⭐ Daily 2-Minute Reset 2 min
On any day you feel tight or stressed: lie on your back, feet up, and do 10 slow breaths. Think belly + ribs expand, pelvic floor drops on the inhale, then just relax on the exhale. This is the fastest way to keep the habit alive even when the full routine doesn't happen.
What success looks like: less clenching, easier belly breathing, better ability to relax on command, and gradually better control. What does not count as progress: just doing more Kegels. Relaxation first, control second.
