⚡ Start here every session: 3-min warmup — jumping jacks, bodyweight squats, arm circles, leg swings. Then hit the circuit below.

Rule: Last 2 reps of every set should be genuinely hard. If not, go heavier or add a rep next time. Rest 60s between sets.

🏋️ The 5 Exercises

1. Goblet Squat 3 × 10-12 · 60s rest
Why: Main leg builder. Hits quads, glutes, core. Biggest muscle groups = most calorie burn per minute.
Hold bell by the horns at your chest. Elbows tucked. Sit back like reaching for a low chair. Heels down. Go below parallel if you can.
2. Single Arm Row 3 × 10-12/arm · 60s rest
Why: Biggest upper body muscle group (back). Counteracts desk posture. Unilateral forces core to work harder.
Hinge at hip. Brace free hand on thigh or chair. Pull bell to your hip, not armpit. Squeeze back at top. Don't twist your torso.
3. Overhead Press 3 × 8-10/arm · 60s rest
Why: Only vertical push. Preserves shoulder strength and upper chest. Key for long-term shoulder health.
First: clean the bell to rack position. Watch the clean here if needed. From rack (bell on forearm at chest), press straight up. Wrist straight. Don't flare elbows. Brace core.
4. Two-Handed KB Swing 3 × 15-20 · 45s rest
Why: Fat-loss weapon. Full-body explosive movement. Torches calories, builds hamstrings, glutes, lower back, grip.
This is a hip hinge, NOT a squat. Hips go back, knees barely bend. Power comes from snapping hips forward. Arms are just ropes. Back stays flat.
5. Reverse Lunge 2 × 8-10/leg · 60s rest
Why: Exposes imbalances. Builds hip stability. Easier on knees than forward lunges. Each leg works harder pound-for-pound.
Hold bell at chest. Step back — not forward. Front knee behind toes. Both knees bend to 90°. Drive through front heel to return. Keep torso upright.

🧘 Pelvic Floor Stretching

When: Tuesday & Thursday · 20 min total
Key: Breathe deep and slow throughout. This is recovery, not training.

🧘 How to work this: Do these in order. 7 stretches. No rushing. Focus on your breathing — slow belly breaths. If your pelvic floor feels different (better, looser, less ache), that's the goal.
1. Deep Squat Hold (Malasana) 2 min
Feet wider than hip-width, toes pointed out slightly. Lower into a deep squat. Heels down if you can — sit on a block or rolled towel if not. Elbows press gently against inner thighs to open the hips. Breathe deep.
2. Child's Pose — Knees Wide 2 min
Knees wide, big toes touching. Sit hips back toward heels. Arms extended forward or resting by your sides. Breathe into your lower back and belly. Relax the pelvic floor on each exhale.
3. Happy Baby 2 min
Lying on your back. Grab the outsides of your feet or shins. Knees wide toward your armpits. Rock gently side to side. This releases inner thighs and the pelvic floor. Breathe.
4. Half-Kneeling Hip Flexor 90 sec / side
Right knee down. Left foot forward at 90°. Squeeze right glute gently and push hips forward until you feel a stretch in the front of the right hip. Hands on left knee for balance. Switch sides.
5. Figure 4 Glute Stretch 90 sec / side
Lying on your back. Cross right ankle over left knee. Pull left thigh toward your chest. Feel the stretch in your right glute and outer hip. Switch sides.
6. Cat-Cow 10 slow breaths
On hands and knees. Inhale — drop belly, lift head and tailbone (cow). Exhale — round spine, tuck chin and pelvis (cat). Move with your breath. Mobilizes pelvis and spine together.
⭐ 7. Diaphragmatic Breathing 5 min
This is the most important one. Lie on your back. Hands on lower belly. Inhale slowly through your nose — belly expands like a balloon. Exhale slowly through your mouth — belly falls. The pelvic floor should relax and lower on the inhale. Do this daily even on KB days.
💡 Don't skip the breathing. The first 6 stretches prepare your body. The 5 minutes of diaphragmatic breathing is where the pelvic floor actually releases. This is not optional.
